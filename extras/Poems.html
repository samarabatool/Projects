Random Boring song

Souuu... I wrote a lot of songs, most of them I can't find. The themes were similar. Anyways, this is a recent one so I could find it.
- August, 2021

The Sun still rises and sets,
The stars still twinkle,
giving me the hope to be that shining guide,
for every lost traveller in the deep black void.

Everything is the same,
Yet my obliviousness creates a hollow in me,
Turning soon into a dark void,
that engulfs all those around me.

The lost traveller,
still walks under the never-ending dark sky,
hoping to make his way through,
fighting all the way with his own dark self.

The pressure of this walk suffocates me,
The void has taken over,
my baggage is getting heavier,
I am losing myself in this nothingness.

The traveller has in himself a void of nothingness,
His burden is eating him away,
suffocating him in foreign lands;
The stars still twinkle above him, unnoticed.

Wrong was not my path nor destiny,
I and I myself was killing me.
My burden is too hard to carry,
It drowns me constantly in oblivion and helplessness.

Thus the wise, valiant traveller,
abandoned his baggage,
collapsing the void,
and filling the hollow in his new self.

I am free again,
from the struggles of carrying my burden,
Guided now by the twinkling stars above me,
Those stars who never left me.

Sorry,
But, yes I do want glory,
You can’t understand me,
Reflect at thee,
Before hurting me.

I am obnoxious, yes I know!
Don’t interrupt me and break the flow
I speak big words,
Yes, I live my worlds!

Trifling with my feelings,
Without knowing their meanings,
Either understand and play,
Or, away you stay!

Know,
The difference between lonely and ‘lone,
Because lying ain’t same as bamboozling,
You ought to respect your offspring,
‘Cause to you they cling.

I poured my heart out in this poem,
And now I have no more complaints from thee,
With a lightened heart now I flee!

An impromptu poem wrote when I was angry with my family. (I was so obsessed with archaic words, its funny)
-October, 2020
There I was,
On railway station at five in the morning,
So early it was,
I lost my sleep and was in mourning.
And Oh! What a disgusting surprise,
The boggy we were in,
Got in the train somehow,
And lost all my exhaustion,
I don't know how.
Streams, and farms and rivers I saw,
Also a cat,
Scratching something with its paw,
Oh! It was an old bat.
Mountains, forests I saw,
On my journey by train,
Tall trees, big bushes I saw,
While following the crow's trail.
And what a fun it was,
Sitting and writing all I saw,
The boggy was the main cause,
To make me remember the day I was scratched by a cat's paw.
Stations came and passed,
Crows and birds flew,
But not even halfway was passed,
As the cold wind blew.
The chaos of people,
The horns of trains,
Filled my ears,
Do they not have brains?
I saw white dandelion,
First time in my life,
I saw a person in train carrying a chandelier,
Jumping out of the moving train with his wife.
After trees, bushes, hills and struggle reached my destination for a good cause,
What an unusual trip it was!
